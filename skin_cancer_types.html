Benign Skin Lesions

Benign skin lesions are non-cancerous growths or abnormalities of the skin. While they do not spread to other parts of the body or pose a serious health risk, they can resemble cancerous lesions and may require evaluation to rule out malignancy.

üß™ Diagnosis:

Diagnosis is primarily clinical, based on appearance. However, when there is doubt, a dermatologist may perform a biopsy to ensure the lesion is not malignant. Dermoscopy (skin surface microscopy) may also be used.

Although benign lesions are harmless, watch for the following warning signs that could indicate a cancerous change:

  • Rapid growth
  • Change in color or shape
  • Itching, bleeding, or crusting
  • Asymmetry or irregular borders

Treatment is often cosmetic or for comfort. Options include:

  • Cryotherapy ‚Äì Freezing the lesion off with liquid nitrogen
  • Shave excision or surgical removal
